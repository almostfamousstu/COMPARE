Understanding Comparisons

Learn how the SKU Comparator determines matches and mismatches, and how to interpret the comparison results effectively.

How Comparisons Work

The Comparison Process

  1. Parse Your Input

    Your specifications are parsed into key-value pairs

  2. Extract Attribute Keys

    Only the attribute names are sent to Google Gemini

  3. Generate AI Specifications

    Gemini generates values for each attribute based on the SKU

  4. Merge and Compare

    Your values and AI values are compared attribute by attribute

  5. Present Results

    Differences are highlighted in the comparison table

Comparison Logic

Value Normalization

Before comparing values, the application normalizes them using this process:

Normalization Steps
1. Trim whitespace: "  Red  " → "Red"
2. Convert to lowercase: "Red" → "red"
3. Compare normalized values

This means:

  • "Red" matches "red"
  • " Blue " matches "blue"
  • "GREEN" matches "green"

Match Determination

Two values are considered a match if:

  • Normalized values are identical
  • Case differences are ignored
  • Leading/trailing whitespace is ignored

Two values are considered a mismatch if:

  • Normalized values differ in any way
  • Different words, even if similar meaning
  • Different formatting or units

Interpreting Results

Perfect Match

When all attributes match, you'll see:

All X attributes match Gemini's response.

This indicates:

  • Strong alignment with AI knowledge
  • Your specifications are consistent with common understanding
  • High confidence in your data accuracy

Some Mismatches

When some attributes differ, you'll see:

X mismatches detected across Y attributes.

This suggests:

  • Review highlighted attributes carefully
  • Some differences may be expected or intentional
  • AI may have different or outdated information
  • Terminology differences might exist

Many Mismatches

If most attributes differ, consider:

  • SKU might be too generic or unclear
  • Product might be custom or specialized
  • AI may lack knowledge about this product
  • Your specifications might use non-standard terminology

Using Comparison Results

Quality Assurance Workflow

  1. Run comparison with your specifications
  2. Review all mismatches in the results table
  3. Categorize differences:
    • Acceptable variations (terminology, units)
    • Potential errors in your data
    • AI inaccuracies or outdated info
  4. Update specifications if errors found
  5. Re-run comparison to verify changes
  6. Document decisions for future reference

Data Validation Workflow

  1. Toggle "Show only differences" to focus on mismatches
  2. Investigate each mismatch:
    • Check source documentation
    • Verify against product specifications
    • Consult with domain experts
  3. Make corrections where needed
  4. Track patterns of common differences
  5. Standardize terminology across your catalog

Limitations to Consider

AI Knowledge Boundaries

  • Training data cutoff: AI may not know about very recent products
  • Niche products: Specialized items may have less accurate data
  • Custom products: Unique or modified items won't match standard specs
  • Regional variations: AI might prefer certain regional terminology

Comparison Algorithm Limitations

  • Exact matching: "256GB" ≠ "256 GB" (space difference)
  • No semantic understanding: "Large" ≠ "Big" even though similar
  • No unit conversion: "1 kg" ≠ "1000 g" even though equivalent
  • No synonym detection: "Smartphone" ≠ "Mobile Phone"

When to Trust the AI

AI responses are more reliable when:

  • Product is well-known and widely available
  • SKU clearly identifies a specific product
  • Attributes are standard industry terms
  • Product has been on market for a while

When to Trust Your Data

Your specifications are more reliable when:

  • Product is custom or specialized
  • You have access to official specifications
  • Product uses non-standard terminology
  • Regional or industry-specific terms are used

Best Practices

For Accurate Comparisons

  • Use specific, detailed SKUs
  • Include brand names when relevant
  • Use standard industry terminology
  • Be consistent with units and formatting
  • Verify unexpected mismatches with source documentation

For Meaningful Results

  • Focus on key differentiating attributes
  • Don't overload with too many attributes (keep to 5-20)
  • Use attributes that have objective values
  • Avoid subjective terms like "high quality" or "best"

For Effective Review

  • Start with "Show only differences" toggled on
  • Investigate all highlighted mismatches
  • Toggle off to verify all attributes reviewed
  • Document reasons for acceptable differences
  • Standardize terminology based on findings

⚠️ Important

The SKU Comparator is a tool to assist with validation and quality assurance. Always verify critical specifications against official documentation. AI responses should be treated as suggestions, not absolute truth.

Privacy Note: Your specification values are never sent to Google Gemini. Only attribute keys (names) are shared, ensuring your proprietary data remains private while still getting valuable AI-generated comparisons.
